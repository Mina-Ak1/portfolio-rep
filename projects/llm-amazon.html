How do LLMs and classical methods classify Amazon reviews?

TL;DR. I built a balanced 6-class dataset (5,000 reviews per category) and compared TF-IDF baselines (Logistic Regression, Random Forest) with a fine-tuned DistilBERT. DistilBERT performs best overall, but the Office Products vs Electronics boundary remains tricky. Short, generic reviews are the hardest for all models.

Categories: Beauty & Personal Care, Books, Electronics, Grocery & Gourmet Food, Toys & Games, Office Products.

Data source: Amazon Reviews 2023 (McAuley Lab)

ModelTrain AccuracyTest Accuracy
Logistic Regression (TF-IDF)0.870.84
Random Forest (TF-IDF)0.860.80
DistilBERT (fine-tuned)0.950.88

Among all categories, Electronics and Office Products have the highest number of false positive cases, while Grocery and Gourmet Food shows the lowest false positives, especially in the fine-tuned DistilBERT model. This contrast makes them a good set for comparison when visualizing how mixed the categories appear after PCA transformation.
